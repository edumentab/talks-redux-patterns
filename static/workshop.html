Is Redux slightly scary? Or you've wrapped your brain around it, but think of it as verbose and boilerplaty? Maybe you just want to get inspired on how to use it and what's possible? If the answer to any of these questions is yes, then this workshop/lecture is for you!

Together we will explore the source code of a small example React application that consumes a backend API and uses Redux for state management. At the outset the setup is rather orthodox, which gives you a chance to get up to speed with a normal Redux setup.

Then, as the workshop progresses, you will be taken through a series of increasingly unorthodox refactors. Our hope is that this will both provide you with new ideas and make you more comfortable with Redux in general!

Special care will be given to the question of where to put our side effect code. We'll dissect Redux Thunk to see how it works, and then kick it out in favour of a better pattern (without having to go üçå with Redux Observables or Redux Sagas).

While Redux is the focus, we'll also get the opportunity to talk about TypeScript concepts (to the extent that the audience so wishes). There will also be a fair bit of discussion around unit testing.

This is an in-depth lecture/workshop for senior and knowledge-hungry JavaScript developers. The primary audience is teams that already use Redux and want to better themselves, but the contents can be tailored to also give a Redux introduction if needed.

The minimal form is a ~2 hour lecture. To make for a more practical experience the lecture can be followed by a refactoring session where we help the team apply the patterns they've learned in their Redux codebase. This can extend the workshop all the way up to a full day.

Not being as formal as one of our regular courses, the Redux workshop works very well as an inspirational evening seminar, or as an "exploration day" serving both as entertainment and competence development.

If this sounds like a good fit for your team, please contact us at info@edument.se!
